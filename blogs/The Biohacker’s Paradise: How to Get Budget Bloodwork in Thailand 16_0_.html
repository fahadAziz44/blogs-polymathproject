The Biohacker’s Paradise: How to Get Budget Bloodwork in Thailand

I just finished a round of blood testing in Chiang Mai, Thailand.

A dozen tests for key biomarkers of health, performance and aging plus a complete allergy panel (70+ tests). All for less than 25% of prices back home.

You can get all sorts of high quality tests (or operations, if that’s your thing) done in Thailand at a fraction of time and price it would cost back home.

Anyone interested in optimizing performance should be tracking biomarkers.The problem is, blood work costs thousands of dollars back home.

For example, compare costs for a complete allergy profile:

  • In the US: $318
  • In Thailand: $75

That’s over $200 in savings for a single test.

Benefits of testing in Thailand:

  • Extremely safe and high quality
  • Prices at less than 25% of back home
  • Fast results in your inbox (often within 24 hours)
  • No wait time (I was in and out in 15 minutes)

If you’re considering comprehensive testing, here’s how the prices can differ:

  • The cost of comprehensive blood work plus allergy panel: $2000-$5000
  • The cost of the same tests in Thailand: $500-$1000

That’s thousands of dollars saved.

Why not use that money and add in a vacation?

Even with a flight to Thailand, you’re still saving hundreds. That’s enough to pay for several weeks of good fun and get your blood work done. (For vanity purposes, I recommend getting tested before the partying starts.)

Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Cost of plane ticket from San Francisco, California: $600-$900.
  • Cost of testing: $500-$1000
  • Cost of accommodation (one week) at a nice hotel: $200-$500
  • Cost of food: $150-$250

Total base cost: $1450-$2650

Where to get your tests

If you want to get tests done, I recommend you avoid labs and go directly to the labs. Wait times are shorter in the labs, and the prices are up to 50% cheaper.

The lab I use: Bria Labs

What tests to get

There are a lot of tests, and I suggest you do your own research (everyone should have a basic education in this stuff), but some things to look at for starters:

  • Complete Blood Count (CBC)
  • Thyroid function (TSH, Free T3, Free T4, TPO)
  • Lipid panel: (cholesterol, triglycerides, LDL, HDL, VLDL)
  • Hormones: (Total Testosterone, Free Testosterone, Estradiol, GH stimulation tests, IGH-1, SHBG)
  • Inflammation markers: Homocysteine, HbA1c
  • Vitamin D (OH test, not the older stuff)
  • Magnesium (red blood cell, not serum)
  • Zinc (red blood cell, not serum)

Also, here are some heuristics if you don’t know what to test:

  • Are you getting 1+ hour of exposure to sunlight on a daily basis?
    • If not, you are probably deficient in Vitamin D.
  • Do you wake up in the morning with an erection? (Men only, obviously)
    • If not, you probably have low testosterone
  • Do you wake up every morning with vivid dreams?
    • If not, you are probably deficient in magnesium

If you’re running on a budget and only get a few tests, here is a question you can ask:

  • What are the 20% of tests that will “catch” 80%+ of problems?
    • For example, only testing total testosterone could be a budget substitute for an entire hormonal panel. You might also want to look at high impact areas where the majority of the population is deficient (Vitamin D, tec.)

Happy testing 🙂
