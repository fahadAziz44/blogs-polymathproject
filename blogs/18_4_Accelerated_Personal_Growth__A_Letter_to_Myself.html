Accelerated Personal Growth: A Letter to Myself

You’re writing a letter to the past.

To teenage you.

Here’s your goal: give advice to maximize personal growth.

What would you write?

###

Hey Charles,

You’re unhappy and don’t have a girlfriend. Heck, you don’t even have friends. Don’t worry about that for now. Those things will come. For now, follow these directions…

Do the following in this order:

  1. Learn how to metalearn. Don’t study “business”, study how to learn. You can be decent at anything in 20 hours or less. Master the process first.
  2. Master social skills. Who you spend time with defines who you will become. Read Carnegie’s book. Practice. Build real friends and ignore the fake ones. Skip class and go to parties. Be vulnerable. You can’t do it all alone.
  3. Build a breadwinner. You’ve got 2/3 core skills. Now you need funding. Find contract work that pays $50+ an hour. Programming is good. Writing is good. Get to the top 5% and be likeable—money won’t be an issue. Work for free at first. Then don’t. Use this to fund the rest of your life.
  4. Surround yourself with masters. You’ve got money and social skills. Find the top 1% of people that inspire you and follow them everywhere. If they have workshops, go to them. If they run a company, join it. If they’re in another country, buy the frickin’ plane ticket and go see them. Every hour with them is worth thousands.
  5. Live life in experiments. Live life in 2-week experiments and 3-month projects. You can’t predict the future. People change. You won’t know what you like. You’ll learn faster and have more fun this way. Never dig your own grave.
  6. Enjoy. Lastly, don’t forget to enjoy the ride. You’re developing because it makes your life better. Don’t miss the NOW in exchange for LATER. Have a good run, my boy.

Don’t settle for average. Think for yourself. Have a wonderful journey.

Love you bro,
Charles

PS Cut your hair. It looks terrible.
