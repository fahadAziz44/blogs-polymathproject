Don’t Pay Me! Why I Work For Free (And You Should Too)

When I saw Kevin’s message, I did a little happy dance.

“I’m emailing you on behalf of the New York Observer…”

The New York Observer gets 6 million readers a month.  Kevin was emailing to ask  if he could syndicate one of my pieces for publication..

Of course, I said yes.

My article hit the front page. That’s my work on display in front of 6 million members of an educated readership.

But attention doesn’t pay the bills. A freelance writer might make $300+ for such a piece. How much did they pay me?

Nothing. Nada. Zero.

Should I be outraged?

Nope. In fact, that’s exactly what I wanted.

If the Observer had offered me 10x the typical payment ($3000), I still would’ve turned them down. I’m here to play the long game. And in the game of writing, there’s a lot more to winning than cold, hard cash.

 The Other Currencies

Money isn’t the only currency.

In exchange for forfeiting cash, writers get something much more valuable…

They get readership.

In exchange for my “free labor”, the Observer let me slip in a little somethin’ somethin’ at the end of my article…

Charles publishes The Open Circle, a free weekly newsletter to 3000+ readers where he deconstructs high-achievers (like the Navy SEALs) and shares exclusive lessons from his own crazy experiments. Join here.

Say hello to the byline—this little beauty is worth ten times any paycheck.

Readers who follow the links are whizzed away on a one-way ride back to my personal webpage… That single article brought over 4000 new readers to my site.

Guess what new readers who already read something they like do when they arrive?

They join my tribe.

Better Than Cash: The Value of a Tribe

I’ll take 4000 readers over $300 in cash any day.

Of those 4000 readers that visited from the Observer article, over 60% of them signed up for The Open Circle, my free weekly newsletter. That’s thousands of new eyes on the best content I produce each week.

Many of these readers go on to become a writer’s most loyal audience. They’ll share your work, give valuable feedback, buy anything you produce in the future and even drive hundreds of miles to come meet you in person.

Who wouldn’t take that over $300 in cash?

I don’t think of my readers as dollar signs, but some big-wig marketers value each “tribe member” at as much as $1 a month. By those standards, those 2000+ signups I got for working “for free” are worth over $24,000 a year!

That’s not the only benefit of a dedicated readership. Cultivating a tribe has a host of other benefits:

  • A negotation lever with publishers for future book deals
  • A “personal network” that you can reach out to for help or collaborate with on future publications
    • Your tribe naturally attracts smart, like-minded thinkers. This can be the start of all sorts of future projects.
  • Smoother pitching to other A-list bloggers and writers
  • A “foot-in-the-door” for features in other prestigious publications

My recommendation to writers: put the money aside and obsess over your tribe instead. Once you have them, you’ll never worry for money again.
